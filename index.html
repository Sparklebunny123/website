Origami

Origami Origami is where you fold paper to beautiful shape that might be complicated or simple. Some have 8 to 12 steps, but some have 80 to 120 steps. Some people even become professional paper folders.

Akira Yoshizawa is often called the father of origami, at his 20s he start to devote himself to origami. Though he made very little money he spent lot of time and energy to his hobby his love of origamini didn't stop. The next 20 years of his live was spent in to proverty. He lived off of selling tsukudani( a traditional pickle with seaweeds) Before him origami was a childhood craft but Akira Yoshizawa made many tecniques like the wet paper tecniqu, which is to damp the paper so it is more pliable. In the 1983 Akira Yoshizawa estimated he made 50,000 models and never sold one. Akira Yoshizawa died in 2005. Eric Joisel followed Akira Yoshizawa like many other. He make complex models. Sometime he would spend more than 100 hour on one model, his models sometime look to be breathing. Each modle is unique, some are exhibited. Over all, origamin has advanced from Akira Yoshizawa, before him origamin was a simple 10 to 20 step process but under him the numbers increase to hundreds or thousands.
